The Situation

Agency FiveEighty is a New York creative agency working across broadcast, digital, and brand. My work here ran across motion graphics, brand identity, competitive display advertising, and event photography for three of the agency's clients: FISLL, the New York Jets, and Spectrum. Early career, but the client list and production standards were not.

The Work

FISLL / Motion & Photography

FISLL is a values-driven athletic brand producing consistent video content with no brand bookends. Every piece went out without a visual identity at open or close.

Produced two sequences in After Effects. The outro: a fire-based logo reveal built on FISLL's circular emblem, energetic without abandoning the mark. The intro: a gritty street-level sequence combining bold animated typography with live-action footage, designed to open with presence and intent. Both built to work at full length and cut down for social without re-editing. Also produced five Instagram video posts and full event photography, 78 edited final images from a single shoot.

New York Jets / Digital Display

Produced a responsive digital display campaign for the New York Jets, built for stadium screens and digital channels, covering a Jets v Colts game-day promotion and the Pigskin Pick Em series. Delivered across desktop and mobile formats optimized for in-venue and programmatic display.

Same campaign across desktop and mobile, including the Pigskin Pick Em open and selected states.

Spectrum / Competitive Conquest

Produced a full competitive display campaign for Spectrum targeting named competitors in regional markets, 33 ads total across desktop, mobile, and tablet. Each ad built against a specific competitor: ATT Gig, Google Fiber, Frontier Fios, Buckeye, Giggle, Ashland, Allo, and others. Designed to intercept audiences actively comparing internet service providers in markets where Spectrum and those competitors were head to head.

33 ads. One per competitor. Built to intercept buyers already in consideration.

Three of the 33 named-competitor variants, each built for a specific market matchup.

Result

A 33-ad competitive conquest system delivered across three breakpoints, each variant built against a named competitor in a defined regional market, the kind of high-intent targeting that intercepts audiences already in active consideration.
