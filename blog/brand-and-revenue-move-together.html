Why Brand and Revenue Have to Move Together at a Launch

JMTA · SPORTIMEGo-to-market · launch strategy6 min read

The traditional model of brand first, revenue second assumes a luxury of time most businesses don't have. Build awareness for six months, then flip on performance marketing. That worked in an era of longer sales cycles and lower competitive pressure. It's increasingly obsolete. At the JMTA expansion launch, I was brought into planning early enough to help shape how the facility would generate revenue from the moment it opened.

Brand is not a prerequisite for revenue. It's a multiplier

A good brand makes every revenue-generating activity more efficient. It makes paid advertising more effective because the audience already has a positive association. It makes referrals more credible because the visual and verbal identity reinforce the story happy members are telling their friends.

But brand work can run in parallel with revenue capture from day one. You don't need a fully established brand before you start trying to sell something. You need enough of a brand to make the thing credible.

Pre-launch creative converts, it doesn't just inspire

One of the most important assets in a facility launch is the pre-opening membership offer. This is not awareness content. It's direct-response creative with a deadline. The identity needs to be compelling, but the copy needs to be specific: what does a founding member get, why act before opening day, and what's the clear path to signing up?

I treat pre-launch membership creative as a separate category from brand storytelling. Different objectives, different metrics, different success criteria.

Many campaigns peak at launch and then go quiet. That quiet is a mistake.

Post-launch creative has a different job

Launch creative generates awareness and captures early demand. Post-launch creative maintains momentum, converts the people who were aware but not yet ready, and builds the community that makes the brand durable.

The weeks after a launch are when curiosity peaks and when the people who watched from a distance are most likely to convert. Keeping a steady drumbeat going in that window, member highlights, stories, program announcements, is how you sustain the lift the launch created. At JMTA, that approach helped the new facility reach roughly 65% court utilization within 90 days of opening.
